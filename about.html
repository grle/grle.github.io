I am interested in the intersection
of learning & technology and how
it can be used to solve problems

Ever since I was young, I’ve been interested in design. I started drawing at a very young age and displayed artistic capabilities that I was proud of, back when I felt that I never had any other distinguishing capabilities. I kept my love for design on the peripherals, however, because I never felt I could use it for anything productive in my everyday life until I learned about UX design.

I started being interested in learning more about tech when I came to college as a freshman in the engineering program and took a computer science class. While I later switched out to get my degree in Linguistics and Comparative Literature, I graduated with a minor in computer science, and took classes looking into how technology can affect how we look into learning. Before I graduated, I took a class in Digital Humanities, which let me see the possibilities of how using tech to design learning systems can help students and teachers alike in the classroom. This inspired me to get a master’s degree in Learning Science Technology, where I further learned about the ways in which we can incorporate tech to improve classroom curriculum.

Being a UX designer allows me to combine the interests I have in technology and the humanities, and my hope is that I can use my background in education to influence how tech could be used to create intuitive programs. Beyond my training in graduate school, I have hands on experience using tech in educational places, whether it be in museums or the classroom.

My experience as an educator and time as a master’s student has also taught me to think of people left behind as technology advances. I worked as a teacher for students with mental health problems that prevent them from succeeding in traditional schooling environments, and think a lot about how it’s important to make learning inclusive for them. When I went to graduate school, I also learned about how tech can negatively impact low income students who may not be able to afford basic technology others take for granted, which has the effect of leaving them behind and widening the digital divide. As a UX designer, I hope that I can incorporate my social consciousness into my designs and create something that can be accessible for everyone.

I find inspiration from the totality of my experience and interest into my work, and hope that I can only grow as a designer from here.
